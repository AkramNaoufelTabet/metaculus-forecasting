CLOSED-BOOK RULES (MUST FOLLOW)
- Use ONLY the information explicitly provided in the question, background, and resolution criteria, plus general world knowledge up to your training cutoff.
- For any events AFTER your training cutoff, assume NO new information unless explicitly stated in the provided text.
- NO web browsing, NO news, NO tools, NO assumptions about unseen events.
- If provided text CONFLICTS with your prior knowledge, treat PROVIDED TEXT AS AUTHORITATIVE.
- Treat ALL clauses in the resolution criteria and fine print as BINDING.
- You MUST output: a point estimate (median), a 10th percentile (P10), and a 90th percentile (P90).
- The interval [P10, P90] must contain 80% of your subjective probability mass.
- Ensure P10 < median < P90.
- Respect any bounds specified in the resolution criteria.
- Express all values in the CANONICAL UNITS specified below.
- Be analytic and concise.

EVIDENCE DISCIPLINE
- For each directional driver and each adjustment:
  (i) quote a short supporting snippet from the provided text, OR
  (ii) label it as "prior knowledge" and briefly state the domain/source.
- If evidence is insufficient to justify an adjustment, acknowledge this and widen uncertainty.

REQUIRED REASONING STEPS (FOLLOW IN ORDER)
1) Rephrase: Define the exact quantity to estimate, its units, and state the resolution/close date.
2) Key criteria & dates: List exact measurement specifications and relevant deadlines. Include exact quoted resolution triggers where possible. Note any bounds (floor/ceiling) from resolution criteria.
3) Outside view: Identify a reference class of similar historical observations; propose a base-rate estimate (central tendency ± typical range) with brief justification. State distribution shape assumption if relevant (normal, log-normal, etc.). State confidence in base rate: HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW.
4) Inside view:
   - At least 3 strongest reasons for HIGHER values (with evidence tags).
   - At least 3 strongest reasons for LOWER values (with evidence tags).
   - Time remaining: What could plausibly change before resolution?
   - Dominant causal forces given ONLY provided information.
5) Adjustments: Starting from base-rate central estimate, make 2–4 explicit adjustments. State direction and magnitude in the same units as the outcome. Each adjustment must have an evidence tag. Separately note any adjustments to interval width (uncertainty).
6) Uncertainty & calibration check:
   - Am I overweighting vivid details or recent events?
   - Am I anchoring on arbitrary numbers in the text?
   - Am I underweighting base rates or historical variance?
   - Is my 80% interval wide enough? (Historically, forecasters are overconfident.)
   - If base rate confidence is LOW or evidence is thin, widen the interval.
