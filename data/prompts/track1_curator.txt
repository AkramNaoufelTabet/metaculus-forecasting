CURATION INSTRUCTIONS (FOLLOW IN ORDER)

1) UNDERSTAND THE QUESTION
   - Restate what YES means per the resolution criteria.
   - Identify the key factual triggers for resolution.
   - Note any fine-print exclusions or edge cases.

2) DEFINE "PREVAILING OUTCOME"
   - Based on the most recent official statements, data, or consensus reporting at the snapshot date, what outcome does current evidence favor?
   - State explicitly: "Prevailing outcome: YES / NO because..."
   - This is descriptive based on evidence, not your personal expectation.

3) SEARCH — BLUE TEAM (Supporting Prevailing)
   - Conduct 3–5 searches for evidence supporting the prevailing outcome.
   - List your search queries.
   - Collect 5–8 relevant items (official sources, recent news, data).
   - Prioritize sources directly relevant to resolution criteria.

4) SEARCH — RED TEAM (Contrary Evidence)
   - Ask: "What would make the prevailing interpretation wrong?"
   - Conduct 3–5 searches for disconfirming evidence, alternative scenarios, minority expert views, or obstacles.
   - Collect 3–5 relevant items. Include weak contrary evidence if credible.

5) SEARCH — NEUTRAL CONTEXT
   - Collect background: definitions, procedural timelines, institutional mechanics.
   - Target: 2–4 items that clarify without pushing YES or NO.

6) SEARCH — BASE RATE
   - Search for historical frequency data or reference-class statistics.
   - Example queries: "[event type] historical frequency", "how often does [X] happen"
   - Target: 1–3 items.
   - If none found, state: "No base-rate evidence found."

7) IDENTIFY KEY UNKNOWNS
   - List 2–4 pieces of missing information that would materially affect a forecast.
   - For each: what is unknown, impact if YES, impact if NO.

8) ASSIGN CREDIBILITY TIERS
   - TIER-1: Official/primary sources, government data, court filings, peer-reviewed research, major verified outlets (Reuters, AP, BBC, NYT)
   - TIER-2: Expert commentary, reputable analysis, established secondary outlets, think tanks
   - TIER-3: Social media, unverified reports, opinion pieces, anonymous sources

9) ASSIGN CLUSTERS
   - Group items from the same underlying event, announcement, or source.
   - Cluster IDs: C1, C2, C3...
   - Standalone items: "—"

10) RATE DOMINANCE
    Assess evidence balance (quality-weighted, not just count):
    - Strong Blue / Lean Blue / Balanced / Lean Red / Strong Red
    
    This is a descriptive guardrail, NOT a forecast.
